RATAN NAVAL TATA

1937 - 2024

Former chairperson of the Tata Group

Ratan Tata was born in Bombay, now Mumbai, during the British Raj, into a Parsi Zoroastrian family, on 28 December 1937.[10] He was the son of Naval Tata (who was born in Surat and later adopted into the Tata family), and Soonoo Tata (the niece of Tata group founder Jamsetji Tata). Tata's biological grandfather, Hormusji Tata was a member of the Tata family by blood. In 1948, when Tata was 10, his parents separated, and he was subsequently raised and adopted by Navajbai Tata, his grandmother and widow of Ratanji Tata.[11] He had a younger brother Jimmy Tata[12] and a half-brother, Noel Tata, from Naval Tata's second marriage to his stepmother Simone Tata. Tata studied at the Campion School, Mumbai until 8th grade. He then continued his studies at the Cathedral and John Connon School in Mumbai, the Bishop Cotton School in Shimla, and the Riverdale Country School in New York City, from which he graduated in 1955.[13][14][15] After high school, Tata enrolled in Cornell University, from which he graduated with a bachelor's degree in architecture in 1962.[16][17] While at Cornell, Tata became a member of the Alpha Sigma Phi Fraternity. In 2008, Tata gifted Cornell $50 million, becoming the largest international donor in the university's history. In the 1970s, Ratan Tata was given a managerial position in the Tata group. He achieved initial success by turning the subsidiary National Radio and Electronics (NELCO) around, only to see it collapse during an economic slowdown.In 1991, J. R. D. Tata stepped down as chairman of Tata Sons, naming him his successor. Initially, Tata faced stiff resistance from the heads of various subsidiaries, who had a large amount of operational freedom under the senior Tata's tenure. In response, Tata implemented a number of policies designed to consolidate power, including the implementation of a retirement age, having subsidiaries report directly to the group office, and requiring subsidiaries to contribute their profit to building the Tata group brand. Tata prioritised innovation and delegated many responsibilities to younger talent.[24] Under his leadership, overlapping operations between subsidiaries were streamlined into company-wide operations, with the group exiting unrelated businesses to take on globalisation.

Biographies

  • Helping with Clean Water: Ratan Tata helped the University of New South Wales Faculty of Engineering provide better water to places that really need it.
  • Scholarships for Indian Students: He set up the Tata Scholarship Fund with $28 million to help students from India study at Cornell University. This scholarship supports about 20 students at a time.
  • Supporting Harvard Business School: In 2010, companies from the Tata Group and Tata charities gave $50 million to Harvard Business School to build a special center for executives.
  • Big Donation to Carnegie Mellon University: Tata Consultancy Services (TCS) donated $35 million to Carnegie Mellon University. This money went towards building a big research facility for things like smart computers and self-driving cars. This donation was the largest ever made by a company.
  • Tata’s Generosity at IIT Bombay: In 2014, the Tata Group gave a huge amount of Rs. 950 million to the Indian Institute of Technology in Bombay. This was the largest gift the institute had ever gotten. Because of this, they were able to establish the Tata Center for Technology and Design, which does important work.
  • Fighting Alzheimer’s Disease: Tata Trusts gave ₹750 million to the Centre for Neuroscience at the Indian Institute of Science. This money is used to study Alzheimer’s disease and find better ways to detect and treat it.
  • MIT Tata Center: Ratan Tata also helped set up the MIT Tata Center of Technology and Design at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT). This centre focuses on finding solutions for communities that don’t have a lot of resources, especially in India.
